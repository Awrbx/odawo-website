About ODAWO

Observatory Development and Welfare Organization is a local non-profit organization established in 2007 and based in Hargeisa, Somaliland. Its main focus is empowering youth and alleviating poverty through capacity building and service delivery in partnership with the government and other actors.

Vision

ODAWO envisions a prosperous and peaceful Somaliland where all people enjoy equal rights and opportunities.

Mission

ODAWO endeavors to empower and improve the quality of life of the marginalized and vulnerable communities in the least developed parts of the country through welfare, participatory research and development, capacity building, promotion of best practices and partnership building, advocacy, and by launching sustainable and replicable innovative projects.

Core Values

  • Accountability
  • Participation
  • Innovation
  • Collaboration
  • Excellence

ODAWO believes in the power of committed youth individuals and organizations who want to contribute to a sustainable development.

Objectives

ODAWO aims to improve community livelihoods and strengthen social services in the areas of education, water, health, environmental protection, infrastructure, roads, hygiene and sanitation, and empowerment of women and children.

Program Focus Areas

  • Human rights: Empowering communities in knowledge and access to health, water, education, capacity and hygiene.
  • Electricity for All: Provision of sustainable, environmentally friendly power solutions.
  • Community Assets: Building public infrastructure and services.
  • HIV awareness: Community outreach and awareness programs.
  • Emergencies: Coping mechanisms for drought and other emergencies.
  • Environmental protection: Initiatives for environmental improvement and protection.

Organizational Structure

ODAWO has a general assembly, board of directors, executive director, and management team. The board consists of five professionals with diverse backgrounds leading departments of admin/finance, program, and fundraising.

Partnerships

ODAWO works in partnership with government, donors, partners, businesses, institutes and communities to improve quality of life for Somali vulnerable people in Togdheer, Sool and Sanaag regions of Somaliland.
